About

Hello, my name is Kyle Engelmann. I'm a recent Northwestern University graduate with experience in game development and computer graphics. I have experience with modern game engines such as Unity and Unreal Engine 4, and have worked in teams to design, develop, and playtest games both inside and outside the classroom, and have worked with WebGL, Nvidia OptiX, and HLSL to generate various graphical effects.

Doc Sprout's Daycare

Role: Game designer and programmer, environmental artist.

In this project, I worked with a small team of students to create a quirky game about raising "doods" who grow "froot" out of their heads. We went from brainstorming ideas and barely usable code to fleshing out a design and a functioning prototype, all the while learning to incorporate player feedback and iterate on our game.

I contributed to the "dood" AI behavior tree. I programmed a simple lighting model and various visual effects, and also 3D modeled environmental assets. Throughout the project, I learned a lot about using Github effectively, and I gained experience both giving and recieving feedback on games.

Our original design was overly ambitious for an 8 week project, which lead to exhaustion and poor planning. We also had issues giving players proper feedback, leading to confusion and frustration. We ironed out many of our issues through frequent playtesting and constant iteration.

I learned about balancing mechanics with the overall vision for the project, and I grew as a programmer throughout the development process. The project had it's challenges, but by working through them, I was able to become a better programmer and designer.
